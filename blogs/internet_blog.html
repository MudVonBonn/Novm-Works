El internet y tú

Kind of my magnum opus simply for how much time I spent on it. This is the work that inspired me to integrate this blog aspect because reading it online works, but doesnt tell the whole story. One of my favorite parts is that it is physically stored on floppy disks. It was a bit of a hassle to get, the floppy disks being from my dads work place and the drive from a store in the thirds story of a building in the middle of a busy market, but getting it to work on a computer was worse. Most modern computers don't support floppy disks anymore, so I had to get an old windows computer from my dads work again and download a tool to be able to read them. The computer ran suuuuper slow but imo it was worth it. In the process of testing out the formating the floppy disked I broke two of them so I ripped it open.

The story itself was very loosely based on Serial Experiments Lain (my fav show). Not really in it's story but in the tech based astetics. My friend Monica helped me play out the caht section and after asking her if I should make it kinda fucked up, she said "yes, asbolutely", so I went all out considering this was for school. I think I did a pretty good job getting the dissasociating vibe down and think that the gore section is the best written because im not very good at moving along a story but decent at describing complicated feelings.

The first part was more of an after thought but I still tried to not make it boring, so I hope I succeded in making it funny. It also emphisices the meaning of the title, a play on how people thought people would use the internet (atleast acording to old guides) compared to how many use it today
