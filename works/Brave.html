I’m brave, aren’t I?

Chapter 1: Standoff

I stare into the beasts’ eyes, full of determination. I need not be scared, for it is chained to a kin of mine, although neither of them seem to take a liking to my bravery. I try not to flinch as both release their wrath at me, first the beast, then its master. Despite the beasts’ struggle, they move away into the distance and turn a corner. I breathe out and let myself feel proud for facing another one of my fears. I’ve made incredible strides regarding this matter, applying basic hygiene in fear of what the others would think of me without it, venturing out into the vast and unpredictable outside and just now facing off against a foul monster.
The gentle wind blows on my face and hands. It feels nice. Although I’ve heard from more experienced venturers that it brings illnesses with it, I don’t particularly care right now. Still, I put on as many clothes on that are fit for the climate as possible. My caretaker had made me a winter hat but not any gloves, but complaining I am not, the hat is cute. Around 10 seconds after surviving the dog and me standing in place contemplating my prior achievements, I quickly spin around 110 degrees with one leg raised in the air and start walking again. Only a few more (hopefully empty) blocks and I’ll take on my biggest challenge yet.

Chapter 2: convenience store

Only about 50 meters away, the fluorescent lights emanating from the building guide me. The postmodern creation of air polluting, open all day convenience stores might be dystopian to some, but bring me a sense of comfort. I am already accustomed to the brightness, and the absence of any other major sign of life makes it feel like a place of refuge for sleepless like me. Just before entering, I mentally prepare myself for a disruption to my otherwise calm state: whatever the crunchy ass bell sound is called that plays when you activate the doors motion sensors. After few steps the sliding doors invite me in, but even after entering no sound plays. Must be broken. Although it does bother me that it didn’t go off even though I specifically awaited it, I try to move on and just get what I came here for.
...
What did I come here for? I guess I’ll buy myself some snacks, but I expected to have come up with something more specific on the way here. As I spend more and more time browning through the snack Isle, I start to feel the pressure to just pick something an get it over with, so plain potato chips It is. Walking to the register I muster up the courage to lift my head from the ground and look at the clerk.
“Cash or Card?” he asks
“ehm, Card!” I reply while fishing for my wallet in my right pocket. After finding and opening it, I come to a horrifying realization, I left the card back home. I can feel the 17 ish year old boy staring at me, waiting for me to hand him my card.
“ah, emmm, I-I…” I stutter'
“Yes?”
“A-actully, I have to..”
Silence fills the room as he waits for me to ginish my sentence
“ I’ll e-emm.. p-pay with cash………….”
“Sure”
I give him a 10 dollar bill, which he checks under the flickering light above him, before putting it in the cash register and handing me my change.
“S-sorry” I mutter under my breath
“excuse me? I didn’t catch that” he says while looking at me with a corporate mandated smile.
I freeze for a few seconds, not daring to look him into the eyes before taking my chips scurrying out.
I manage to not think about the interaction until I reach the front door of my house. What the fuck is wrong with me. How did I mess that up so badly. I want to crawl into a ditch and die. Im so fucking pathetic I want everyone gone. As my vision starts to blur I enter my home, wipe away the tears forming under my eyes, ignore my mother and make my way into my room to lay on the ground.
